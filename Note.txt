1.  Scaling

    Scaling in mongoDB refers to the process of increasing or decreasing the resources allocated to your database
    to meet changing demands.

    # Horizontal Scaling

        It also called Scaling out, it involves adding more servers to your database cluster.

    # Vertical Scaling

        increasing the resources of a single server, like RAM , CPU.

2.  Shardding

    Shardding is a database Scaling technique that involves distributed data across the multiple servers. This allow you
    to scale your database Horizontaly.

3. Replication

    Replication is the process of creating and maintaining copy of data into the multiple servers.
    it imporves redundancy, availability, scalability
